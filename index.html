Delia Derbyshire

The woman behind the Doctor Who Theme

Delia Derbyshire cutting tape (source: https://www.youtube.com)

Some of the stations in Delia's life:

  • born in Coventry, England, in 1937
  • BA Mathematics and Music in 1959, Girton College, Cambridge
  • joined BBC as trainee assistant studio manager in November 1960
  • was assigned to BBC Radiophonic Workshop in 1962
  • created Doctor Who theme in 1963, after score by Ron Grainer
  • additional, notable works:
    • Interventions for Radio with Barry Bermange
    • The Cyprian Queen, 1964
    • Ziwzih Ziwzih OO-OO-OO, 1986
  • released debut album An Electric Storm with David Vorhaus and Brian Hodgson as the band White Noise
  • after her death in 2001, an archive of hundreds of tapes and thousands of papers was found in her house and is now on permanent loan at the University of Manchester
"I'm dead keen on limiting resources [...] You've got to impose some discipline on either the form you're going to use or the sounds you're going to use.[...] If you cannot discipline yourself... You'd end up... "Oh I like this, I like that, I want it all at once."
Delia Derbyshire, Surface magazine, May 2000
